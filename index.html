Shimla, also known as Simla, is the capital and largest city of the northern Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. In 1864, Shimla was declared as the summer capital of British India, succeeding Murree, northeast of Rawalpindi. After independence, the city became the capital of Punjab and was later named the capital of Himachal Pradesh. It is the principal commercial, cultural and educational centre of the hilly regions of the state.

The Ridge

Historic pedestrian plaza surrounded by Raj-era architecture, scenic views, and cultural landmarks. Ideal for leisurely strolls and soaking up the local atmosphere.

Christ Church

Historic cathedral with serene neo-Gothic architecture, tranquil atmosphere for reflection, and stained-glass windows. Regular services and cultural memorials on The Ridge

Indian Institute of Advanced Study

Historical site with guided tours through a grand 1884 building, showcasing India's independence era. Features stunning architecture and well-maintained gardens

Green Valley

The Green Valley has magnificent views of thousands of Pine & Deodar trees on the hill slopes.

Jakhu Hanuman Temple

Elevated journey via cable car leads to a serene temple with a towering 108 ft Hanuman statue, amid panoramic city views and playful monkeys.

The Mall

Pedestrian-friendly shopping district intermingled with heritage buildings and scenic vistas. Features local handicrafts, diverse dining options, and leisure activities amidst historical ambiance.

Viceregal Lodge

Historic site with Scottish baronial architecture and Burma teak interiors, featuring guided tours of its significant role in Indian history and limited access to private areas.

Jakhoo Hill

Jakhoo Hill is the highest point in Shimla, India, known for its panoramic views and the iconic Jakhoo Temple, which houses a 108-foot statue of Lord Hanuman.
